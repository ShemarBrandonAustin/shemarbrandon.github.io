My Blender Projects

My First Character Attempt

This was my first attempt at creating a character. It was a cowboy with no name. The reference image that was being used was provided by the person who was teaching me this (Whose name i cant remember). The way he made it was by resizing shapes (mainly a cube) to fit the length and width of the character. While his method could have worked fine it would only work for low level renders and that would be it. Since i planned to use these character to animate one day this was not an ideal method for me to use for modelling a character.

My Attempt at creating a Character Head

This was my attempt at creating a Head. This is a long and painstaking process especially for beginners and there aren't enough tutorials out there to teach you. The Photo above shows how far i got with making a face on my own which is complicated in its own right but making the head might be so much harder. Not to mention to make a face that can animate you need to make it according to muscles in a real human face to create accurate speech patterns.

My Attempt at creating a Character Body

There were many ways of going about doing this but maybe the best i've found is by reshpaing a cylinder to fit the reference body. This works better with female bodies with more curves. To make this compatible for animation as well is a long process but it is probably the easiest (This includes making hands and toes and even clothes). This is one of the first things i learnt to make for character modelling and although i do not have my original shots of the process this is one i never completeed.

Creating a Make-Shift Studio

Renders only look realisitc if they have everything a real world would have and one of these things are backgrounds. This attempt at a "Studio" was made so i have a place to put finished projects and render them to make them look as if they had their photo taken in a real life studio room. This project went incompleted because HDRs came into the mix. With HDRs i would no longer have to make an entire bacground or even do lighting HDRs do all of that buy taking real world pictures 360° and placing them into the render to take its lightsource and background
